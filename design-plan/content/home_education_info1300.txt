I am currently a student at Cornell University, pursuing a double major in Economics and Information Science with a concentration in Data Science. My coursework spans economics, mathematics, data science, and information science. Despite the technical nature of my courseload, I have had the opportunity to collaborate with peers on many technical group projects that have helped me develop stronger teamwork skills and learn to communicate technical concepts to people of different levels. Two classes that have been especially impactful are INFO 2950 (Introduction to Data Science) and ECON 3120 (Applied Econometrics). Both courses showed me how there is so much more to data and its applications in the world than I had ever known before. I learned to think carefully about how data is collected, modeled, and interpreted, and to question the assumptions behind empirical results.
